Tips for maintaining a healthy sleep pattern

Healthy sleep is a regularly recurring condition of the body and brain, characterized by reduced waking consciousness, decreased sensory activity, lowered muscle activity and motor coordination during REM (rapid eye movement) sleep, decreased associations with surrounding stimuli, and increased time awake. As a result, healthy sleep is desirable in almost every individual and requires relatively less effort to achieve than does insomnia. Sleep quality is enhanced when one's routine is regular and soothing, and there is adequate time to repair one's brain cells and rest the brain and spinal cord. Numerous studies over the years have found that the average individual needs about 8 hours of good quality sleep each night in order to be healthy.

In order to have a healthy sleep, it is important to maintain the routine of the day. This means that the bedtime routine must always conform to the natural body clock and involve the scheduling of sleep, wake up cues, relaxing activities and food choices, etc. It is also critical that the routine of the day, including bedtime routines, be con sistent throughout the entire waking day and not simply change at random times during the evening. Although people's sleep patterns naturally vary from day to day, setting appropriate bedtime, taking naps and using relaxation techniques during the day, is extremely important. This will reduce the probability of experiencing prolonged and disruptive sleep interruptions, which are the most common reason for poor sleep hygiene.

Another important aspect of having a healthy sleep is ensuring that the immune syst em is functioning properly. Regularly sleeping and resting in bed helps the immune system to perform at its best, and this has been shown to reduce the likelihood of illness and disease outbreaks throughout the daily course of life. The immune system natur ally destroys bacteria, viruses, and other impurities in the body's bloodstream on a daily basis. When a person sleeps and remains inactive, the immune system cannot take in as many foreign and dangerous agents into the body's cells. Therefore, if an indiv idual does not take part in their normal daily activities, and does not get enough sleep at night, their immune system will be weakened over time.

In addition, a healthy sleep schedule promotes a strong and stable body weight, due to the fact that it regulates metabolism and causes the body to feel full quickly after a meal. It also encourages the release of energy and nutrients into the blood, which enables you to function efficiently through the day. Individuals who do not have a regular and consistent bedtime are often more inactive throughout the day, and therefore are at a higher risk of becoming overweight or obese. Therefore, keeping to a regular bedtime schedule will not only promote better overall health, but will also prevent the onset of chronic h ealth conditions such as diabetes, high blood pressure, heart disease, osteoporosis, stroke, and even certain forms of cancer.

Furthermore, people who are regular sleepers often report feeling less "angry" and "stressful" throughout the day. Those who are not regular sleepers are much more likely to experience feelings of frustration, anger and stress. Unfortunately, when these same individuals do not get enough sleep at night, they are likely to find it much harder to cope with stress in their lives. They are also much more likely to experience depression. Lack of sleep can also dramatically increase the occurrence of accidents, since many individuals who do not get enough sleep at night become extremely sleepy during daytime activities. Finally, individuals who are unable to get enough sleep are much more likely to be late sleepers, or to wake up feeling tired in the morning and lethargic.

If you have a healthy sleep pattern, you will be better equipped to face the day, especially if your lifestyle includes a very regular work pattern, irregular sleep hours, or inconsistent sleeping hours. You will also find that you are more alert and energetic. The following tips will help you get and maintain the right amount of sleep:

Try to determine your ideal bedtime and start times for your sleeping hours. If you are unsure about your ideal bedtime or start time, try making a few experiments. For example, if you know your favorite television show is on, try to find a time on that day that is most similar to your favorite show's start time. Or, if you like to watch sports on TV, try to find a time on that day when the sports are less busy. Or, if you are unable to sleep at all, try to establish a regular bedtime and wake time. If you cannot sleep after one week of consistent sleeping habits, you should probably consider changing your sleep habits!

Another great way to maintain a healthy sleep pattern is to follow good sleep hygiene. This means that you need to get enough sleep, especially when you wake up. Many people ar e simply not aware that their lifestyle has a strong impact on their sleep hygiene. A lack of sleep can result in irritability, health problems, and a lower quality of life. Following these helpful tips can help you establish a healthy sleep hygiene and enjoy better sleep throughout the week.
